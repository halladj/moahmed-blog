## Why Medication Safety Matters

Medication errors are one of the leading causes of preventable harm worldwide. The World Health Organization estimates that medication-related errors cost **$42 billion annually**. But many of these errors can be prevented with simple precautions.

## Tip 1: Keep an Updated Medication List

Always maintain a current list of all medications you take, including:

- Prescription medications
- Over-the-counter drugs
- Vitamins and supplements
- Herbal products

Bring this list to every doctor visit and pharmacy encounter.

## Tip 2: Understand Your Medications

For each medication, know:

- **What** it's for
- **How** to take it (with food? at bedtime?)
- **When** to take it
- **What to avoid** (interactions with food, alcohol, or other drugs)

Don't hesitate to ask your pharmacist — that's what we're here for!

## Tip 3: Never Share Medications

A medication prescribed for you may be dangerous for someone else, even if they have similar symptoms. Dosages are tailored to individual factors like weight, kidney function, and other medications.

## Tip 4: Store Medications Properly

- Keep medications in their original containers
- Store in a cool, dry place (not the bathroom — too humid!)
- Keep out of reach of children
- Check expiration dates regularly

## Tip 5: Use One Pharmacy

Using a single pharmacy allows your pharmacist to:

- Track all your medications in one system
- Screen for drug interactions
- Identify potential duplications
- Provide consistent counseling

## Conclusion

Medication safety is a shared responsibility between patients, pharmacists, and the entire healthcare team. These simple steps can significantly reduce your risk of medication-related problems.
