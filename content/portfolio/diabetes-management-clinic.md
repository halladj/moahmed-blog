## Overview

I designed and launched a pharmacist-led diabetes management clinic within a primary care setting. Working under a collaborative practice agreement with physicians, I provided comprehensive medication management for patients with uncontrolled type 2 diabetes.

## The Need

Many patients with type 2 diabetes struggle to achieve glycemic targets due to:

- Complex medication regimens
- Limited appointment time with physicians
- Gaps in self-management education
- Barriers to medication adherence

## Services Provided

- **Medication Therapy Management:** Reviewed and optimized diabetes medications, including insulin titration
- **Patient Education:** Conducted one-on-one counseling on blood glucose monitoring, diet, exercise, and medication use
- **Lab Monitoring:** Ordered and interpreted HbA1c, fasting glucose, and lipid panels
- **Care Coordination:** Communicated with physicians, dietitians, and diabetes educators

## Outcomes (First 12 Months)

- **Patients served:** 215
- **Average HbA1c reduction:** 1.8% (from 9.2% to 7.4%)
- **Patients achieving HbA1c < 7%:** increased from 18% to 47%
- **Patient satisfaction score:** 4.8 / 5.0
- **Medication adherence rate:** improved from 54% to 82%

## Impact

This clinic demonstrated that pharmacist-led chronic disease management can produce measurable, meaningful improvements in patient outcomes. It was later adopted as a model for expanding clinical pharmacy services across the health system.

## Lessons Learned

1. Building trust with patients takes time — consistency is key
2. Collaborative practice agreements unlock the full potential of pharmacist expertise
3. Tracking outcomes is essential for proving value and securing ongoing support
