## Overview

As the lead clinical pharmacist, I spearheaded a comprehensive medication safety improvement program at a 400-bed teaching hospital. The project aimed to reduce preventable medication errors across all inpatient units.

## The Problem

An internal audit revealed that medication errors were occurring at a rate higher than the national benchmark. Key issues included:

- Incorrect dosing, especially for high-alert medications
- Look-alike/sound-alike drug confusion
- Incomplete medication reconciliation at transitions of care
- Inadequate documentation of patient allergies

## My Role

- **Project Lead:** Coordinated a multidisciplinary team of pharmacists, nurses, physicians, and IT staff
- **Data Analyst:** Collected and analyzed error reports to identify root causes
- **Educator:** Developed and delivered staff training programs
- **Policy Developer:** Revised medication-use policies and procedures

## Approach

### Phase 1: Assessment (Months 1-3)
- Conducted a baseline analysis of medication error reports
- Surveyed staff on barriers to safe medication practices
- Benchmarked against national safety standards

### Phase 2: Implementation (Months 4-9)
- Introduced barcode medication administration (BCMA) on all units
- Implemented standardized high-alert medication protocols
- Created a "Do Not Crush" medication list
- Launched a medication reconciliation improvement initiative

### Phase 3: Evaluation (Months 10-12)
- Monitored error rates monthly
- Collected staff feedback on new processes
- Reported outcomes to hospital leadership

## Results

| Metric | Before | After | Change |
|--------|--------|-------|--------|
| Medication errors per 1,000 patient-days | 12.4 | 8.1 | **-35%** |
| Wrong-dose errors | 4.2 | 1.8 | **-57%** |
| Medication reconciliation completion rate | 68% | 94% | **+38%** |
| Staff safety culture score | 62% | 81% | **+31%** |

## Key Takeaways

- Technology alone doesn't solve safety problems — culture change is essential
- Frontline staff engagement is critical to sustainable improvement
- Continuous monitoring and feedback loops drive lasting results
