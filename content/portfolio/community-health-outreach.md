## Overview

I initiated and led a community health outreach program focused on medication education, health screenings, and preventive care awareness in underserved neighborhoods.

## Motivation

Many community members face barriers to healthcare access, including:

- Limited access to a pharmacist for medication questions
- Low health literacy
- Financial barriers to preventive screenings
- Distrust of the healthcare system

## What We Did

### Health Screening Events
- Blood pressure checks
- Blood glucose screenings
- BMI assessments
- Medication reviews (brown bag events)

### Educational Workshops
- Understanding Your Medications 101
- Managing Chronic Conditions at Home
- Over-the-Counter Medications: What You Need to Know
- Vaccine Awareness and Myths

### Resource Distribution
- Medication organizers and pill boxes
- Multilingual health education materials
- Lists of affordable pharmacy and clinic resources

## By the Numbers

- **12** community events organized over 18 months
- **500+** community members served
- **120** medication reviews completed
- **45** urgent referrals to physicians based on screening results
- **8** pharmacy student volunteers trained

## Community Feedback

> "I didn't know my blood pressure was so high. The pharmacist helped me understand why I need to take my medicine every day." — Community participant

> "This was the first time someone explained my medications to me in my language." — Community participant

## Lasting Impact

The program established ongoing partnerships with community centers, local health departments, and volunteer organizations. It continues to operate with the help of pharmacy students and community health workers.
